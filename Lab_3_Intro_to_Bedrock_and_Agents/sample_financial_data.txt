The following excerpts are drawn from SEC 10-K annual filings for three major technology companies:
Apple Inc., Microsoft Corporation, and NVIDIA Corporation.

Products and Services

Apple Inc. derives revenue from iPhone, Mac, iPad, Wearables (Apple Watch, AirPods), and
Services (App Store, Apple Music, iCloud, Apple Pay). iPhone remains the largest revenue
contributor, accounting for approximately 52% of total net sales in fiscal year 2024.

Microsoft Corporation operates through three segments: Productivity and Business Processes
(Office 365, LinkedIn, Dynamics), Intelligent Cloud (Azure, SQL Server, Windows Server),
and More Personal Computing (Windows, Xbox, Surface, Search). Azure revenue grew 29%
year-over-year, driven by enterprise cloud migration and AI workload demand.

NVIDIA Corporation designs GPUs and systems-on-chip for two primary markets: Data Center
and Gaming. The Data Center segment, which includes the H100 and A100 accelerators,
generated $47.5 billion in fiscal 2024 revenue, representing 83% of total revenue.

Risk Factors

Apple identifies supply chain concentration in East Asia, foreign exchange fluctuations,
regulatory scrutiny of App Store practices, and dependence on consumer discretionary
spending as material risks. Component shortages have previously constrained iPhone
production volumes.

Microsoft cites cybersecurity threats to cloud infrastructure, competition in enterprise
AI services, regulatory compliance across 190 countries, and talent acquisition in
machine learning and cloud engineering as key risk factors. Azure outages carry
reputational and contractual liability.

NVIDIA faces semiconductor supply constraints through its dependence on TSMC fabrication,
export restrictions on advanced chips to certain markets, customer concentration among
hyperscale cloud providers, and rapid product cycle transitions that risk inventory
obsolescence. Geopolitical tensions affecting Taiwan represent a material supply risk.

Financial Metrics

Apple reported total revenue of $383.3 billion, net income of $97.0 billion, and total
assets of $352.6 billion for fiscal year 2024. The company maintained a gross margin
of 46.2% and returned $94.8 billion to shareholders through dividends and buybacks.

Microsoft reported total revenue of $245.1 billion, operating income of $109.4 billion,
and total assets of $512.2 billion for fiscal year 2024. Cloud services gross margin
improved to 72%, reflecting economies of scale in Azure operations.

NVIDIA reported total revenue of $60.9 billion, net income of $29.8 billion, and total
assets of $65.7 billion for fiscal year 2024. Data Center gross margin reached 76.7%,
driven by strong demand for AI training and inference accelerators.
